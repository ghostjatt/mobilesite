Review Motorola Moto G

Friday, December 27, 2013 » Android, Smartphone

Motorola Moto G review 2013

The Motorola Moto G Motorola Moto G is the company's take on Android, v4.3 (Jelly Bean), upgradable to 4.4.2 (KitKat) phones with 4.5 inches, 720 x 1280 pixels inches screen. The handset giant is advertising the phone with many efforts. There's also a major shift in terms of the build of the phone and in some other attributes.

We will try to find in this review that whether this phone can make it in the top lead of Android, v4.3 (Jelly Bean), upgradable to 4.4.2 (KitKat) mobile phones.

Build/ Design

When seen from far, it appear as a regular mobile phone, but a closer look proved it this new Motorola Moto G is pretty well designed and slick phone. Motorola Moto G has better design than Motorola Motorola Moto G and XOLO Xolo A500s IPS, and it even feels more compact. The phone has sexy rounded edges. It's available in different colour variants which might feel good to some people.

The front of the Motorola Moto G features a 4.5 inches, 720 x 1280 pixels 5-inch display and the earpiece, and front camera , notification LED and the sensor array above it.

[Motorola Moto G]()

Motorola Moto G features the Power / Screen lock key and the Volume rocker key at the right hand side, and two micro-SIM trays at the left side. The Micro-USB port sits at the top edge of the phone, while the 3.5mm headset jack is located at the bottom edge.

The back of the Motorola Moto G is dominated by a cover which is flanked by two plastic strips at the top and bottom. The Motorola Moto G houses the 5 MP with autofocus-megapixel camera lens that protrudes out a little, and a big LED flash .

The bottom strip houses the speaker grill of the phone. There's some branding at the middle of the Motorola Moto G in the form of a logo.

Overall, the build quality of the Motorola Moto G can be considered the best among all other phones of similar range and category. The phone is well-built, exudes a feeling of durability and feels nice to hold. Despite sporting a 4.5 inches, 720 x 1280 pixels-inch display, the phone is perfect for one-hand use.

Display of Motorola Moto G

The Motorola Moto G comes with a 4.5 inches, 720 x 1280 pixels-inch display with pixels resolution and pixel density of ppi.

The screen is one of the phone's headline features, and thanks to that text and images looked crisp and sharp but we were not very impressed with the display's colour reproduction, specially how it displayed blacks. Motorola Moto G's screen also looked a bit dull, and brightness levels were below par if you compare it with phones like the Motorola Motorola Moto G.

Camera of Motorola Moto G

Motorola Moto G supports a 5 MP with autofocus-megapixel rear camera and a True-megapixel front facing camera. offers its support for camera app that includes Normal,
HDR, Smile detection, Panorama and Scene detection shot modes.

In our use, we found the camera of the Motorola Moto G to be a bit off in terms of white balance and it softened the tone to a cooler shade. Images clicked with the phone's rear camera lacked warmth and at times looked very bland. Images taken during the day were still passable but the ones taken in indoor light appeared totally different from the prevailing ambient light conditions.

Overall, the cameras on the Motorola Moto G are strictly average for a phone in this segment.

Software/ User Interface of Motorola Moto G

Motorola Moto G runs Android, v4.3 (Jelly Bean), upgradable to 4.4.2 (KitKat), which is a very recent version of the OS and comes with some skinned elements of the UI like the app icons.

's custom UI layer is not as radical as HTC's Sense UI or Samsung's TouchWiz UI, but does add some customisation and third party apps.

Just like the Motorola Motorola Moto G, the Motorola Moto G also features some software tricks, including a new lock screen mechanism that allows the phone to be unlocked with a blow of air or if the phone is shaken. It works as promised but disables the lock screen widgets, so we just feel it's too gimmicky. It's not enabled by default.

Performance of Motorola Moto G

The Motorola Moto G is powered by a Quad Core 1.2 GHz processor with 1 GB RAM onboard. There is 16 GB of internal storage.

With Android, v4.3 (Jelly Bean), upgradable to 4.4.2 (KitKat), the overall experience of navigation through the interface was pleasant, thanks to the phone's quad-core processor. We did not
experience any lag while launching apps, playing games, scrolling web pages or switching between apps. It is safe to say that performance wise, the Motorola Moto G scores well.

The Motorola Moto G comes with a 2070 mAH, Li-ion Battery battery, and based on our experience, it will last you 250 hours. With medium to heavy usage, including 1-1.5 hours
of phone calls, two e-mail accounts with push notifications, screen at maximum brightness, playing some music and video clips (about an hour), casual web
browsing, Twitter notifications and WhatsApp chats, the phone lasted 1440 minutes.
