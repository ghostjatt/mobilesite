Review Salora SM504

The Salora SM504 Salora SM504 is the company's take on Android, v2.1 phones with 2.4 inches, 240 x 320 pixels inches screen. The handset giant is advertising the phone with many efforts. There's also a major shift in terms of the build of the phone and in some other attributes.

We will try to find in this review that whether this phone can make it in the top lead of Android, v2.1 mobile phones.

Build/ Design

When seen from far, it appear as a regular mobile phone, but a closer look proved it this new Salora SM504 is pretty well designed and slick phone. Salora SM504 has better design than Karbonn Karbonn K20 Plus and Spice Spice Stellar Virtuoso Pro Mi-491, and it even feels more compact. The phone has sexy rounded edges. It's available in different colour variants which might feel good to some people.

The front of the Salora SM504 features a 2.4 inches, 240 x 320 pixels 5-inch display and the earpiece, , notification LED and the sensor array above it.

Salora SM504 features the Power / Screen lock key and the Volume rocker key at the right hand side, and two micro-SIM trays at the left side. The Micro-USB port sits at the top edge of the phone, while the 3.5mm headset jack is located at the bottom edge.

The back of the Salora SM504 is dominated by a cover which is flanked by two plastic strips at the top and bottom. The Salora SM504 houses the VGA (0.3 MP)-megapixel camera lens that protrudes out a little, and a big LED flash .

The bottom strip houses the speaker grill of the phone. There's some branding at the middle of the Salora SM504 in the form of a logo.

Overall, the build quality of the Salora SM504 can be considered the best among all other phones of similar range and category. The phone is well-built, exudes a feeling of durability and feels nice to hold. Despite sporting a 2.4 inches, 240 x 320 pixels-inch display, the phone is perfect for one-hand use.

Display of Salora SM504

The Salora SM504 comes with a 2.4 inches, 240 x 320 pixels-inch display with pixels resolution and pixel density of ppi.

The screen is one of the phone's headline features, and thanks to that text and images looked crisp and sharp but we were not very impressed with the display's colour reproduction, specially how it displayed blacks. Salora SM504's screen also looked a bit dull, and brightness levels were below par if you compare it with phones like the Karbonn Karbonn K20 Plus.

Camera of Salora SM504

Salora SM504 supports a VGA (0.3 MP)-megapixel rear camera . offers its support for camera app that includes Normal,
HDR, Smile detection, Panorama and Scene detection shot modes.

In our use, we found the camera of the Salora SM504 to be a bit off in terms of white balance and it softened the tone to a cooler shade. Images clicked with the phone's rear camera lacked warmth and at times looked very bland. Images taken during the day were still passable but the ones taken in indoor light appeared totally different from the prevailing ambient light conditions.

Overall, the cameras on the Salora SM504 are strictly average for a phone in this segment.

Software/ User Interface of Salora SM504

Salora SM504 runs Android, v2.1, which is a very recent version of the OS and comes with some skinned elements of the UI like the app icons.

's custom UI layer is not as radical as HTC's Sense UI or Samsung's TouchWiz UI, but does add some customisation and third party apps.

Just like the Karbonn Karbonn K20 Plus, the Salora SM504 also features some software tricks, including a new lock screen mechanism that allows the phone to be unlocked with a blow of air or if the phone is shaken. It works as promised but disables the lock screen widgets, so we just feel it's too gimmicky. It's not enabled by default.

Performance of Salora SM504

The Salora SM504 is powered by a 600 MHz Processor 600 MHz Processor processor with 512 MB RAM onboard. There is 128 MB of internal storage.

With Android, v2.1, the overall experience of navigation through the interface was pleasant, thanks to the phone's quad-core processor. We did not
experience any lag while launching apps, playing games, scrolling web pages or switching between apps. It is safe to say that performance wise, the Salora SM504 scores well.

The Salora SM504 comes with a 1500 mAH, Li-ion Battery battery, and based on our experience, it will last you 600 hours. With medium to heavy usage, including 1-1.5 hours
of phone calls, two e-mail accounts with push notifications, screen at maximum brightness, playing some music and video clips (about an hour), casual web
browsing, Twitter notifications and WhatsApp chats, the phone lasted 240 minutes.
