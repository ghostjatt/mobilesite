Review Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562

The Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 is the company's take on Android, v4.0 phones with 4 inches, 480 x 800 pixels inches screen. The handset giant is advertising the phone with many efforts. There's also a major shift in terms of the build of the phone and in some other attributes.

We will try to find in this review that whether this phone can make it in the top lead of Android, v4.0 mobile phones.

Build/ Design

When seen from far, it appear as a regular mobile phone, but a closer look proved it this new Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 is pretty well designed and slick phone. Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 has better design than Fly Fly F451s and Micromax Micromax X456 Hook Up, and it even feels more compact. The phone has sexy rounded edges. It's available in different colour variants which might feel good to some people.

The front of the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 features a 4 inches, 480 x 800 pixels 5-inch display and the earpiece, and front camera , notification LED and the sensor array above it.

Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 features the Power / Screen lock key and the Volume rocker key at the right hand side, and two micro-SIM trays at the left side. The Micro-USB port sits at the top edge of the phone, while the 3.5mm headset jack is located at the bottom edge.

The back of the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 is dominated by a cover which is flanked by two plastic strips at the top and bottom. The Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 houses the 5 MP with autofocus-megapixel camera lens that protrudes out a little, and a big LED flash .

The bottom strip houses the speaker grill of the phone. There's some branding at the middle of the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 in the form of a logo.

Overall, the build quality of the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 can be considered the best among all other phones of similar range and category. The phone is well-built, exudes a feeling of durability and feels nice to hold. Despite sporting a 4 inches, 480 x 800 pixels-inch display, the phone is perfect for one-hand use.

Display of Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562

The Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 comes with a 4 inches, 480 x 800 pixels-inch display with pixels resolution and pixel density of ppi.

The screen is one of the phone's headline features, and thanks to that text and images looked crisp and sharp but we were not very impressed with the display's colour reproduction, specially how it displayed blacks. Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562's screen also looked a bit dull, and brightness levels were below par if you compare it with phones like the Fly Fly F451s.

Camera of Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562

Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 supports a 5 MP with autofocus-megapixel rear camera and a True-megapixel front facing camera. offers its support for camera app that includes Normal,
HDR, Smile detection, Panorama and Scene detection shot modes.

In our use, we found the camera of the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 to be a bit off in terms of white balance and it softened the tone to a cooler shade. Images clicked with the phone's rear camera lacked warmth and at times looked very bland. Images taken during the day were still passable but the ones taken in indoor light appeared totally different from the prevailing ambient light conditions.

Overall, the cameras on the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 are strictly average for a phone in this segment.

Software/ User Interface of Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562

Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 runs Android, v4.0, which is a very recent version of the OS and comes with some skinned elements of the UI like the app icons.

's custom UI layer is not as radical as HTC's Sense UI or Samsung's TouchWiz UI, but does add some customisation and third party apps.

Just like the Fly Fly F451s, the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 also features some software tricks, including a new lock screen mechanism that allows the phone to be unlocked with a blow of air or if the phone is shaken. It works as promised but disables the lock screen widgets, so we just feel it's too gimmicky. It's not enabled by default.

Performance of Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562

The Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 is powered by a 1 GHz Processor 1 GHz Processor processor with 768 MB RAM onboard. There is 4 GB of internal storage.

With Android, v4.0, the overall experience of navigation through the interface was pleasant, thanks to the phone's quad-core processor. We did not
experience any lag while launching apps, playing games, scrolling web pages or switching between apps. It is safe to say that performance wise, the Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 scores well.

The Samsung Galaxy S Duos S7562 comes with a 1500 mAH, Li-ion Battery battery, and based on our experience, it will last you 445 hours, 330 hours on 3G. With medium to heavy usage, including 1-1.5 hours
of phone calls, two e-mail accounts with push notifications, screen at maximum brightness, playing some music and video clips (about an hour), casual web
browsing, Twitter notifications and WhatsApp chats, the phone lasted 780 minutes, 445 minutes on 3G.
